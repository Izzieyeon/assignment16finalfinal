Dakgalbi is a popular Korean dish made with marinated chicken, stir-fried with vegetables and a spicy sauce. It originated in the city of Chuncheon in Gangwon Province, South Korea.

The dish typically consists of bite-sized pieces of chicken marinated in a spicy gochujang (red pepper paste) sauce, along with sweet potatoes, cabbage, scallions, and other vegetables. The ingredients are stir-fried together in a large pan or wok until the chicken is cooked through and the vegetables are tender.

Dakgalbi is often served with rice, lettuce leaves, and other side dishes such as kimchi and pickled radishes. It is also common to add cheese to the dish, which is melted on top of the dakgalbi towards the end of the cooking process.
