This is one of the comfort food of my family! My mom usually cook this when we are sick. I always feel better after eating the steamy hot Samgye-tang when I am sick.

Samgyetang is a popular Korean soup made with a whole young chicken stuffed with glutinous rice, Korean ginseng, and garlic, and then simmered in a broth with various herbs and spices. The dish is traditionally served during the hot summer months in Korea as it is believed to provide energy and stamina to combat the heat.

The chicken used for samgyetang is typically small and young, and the rice stuffing is meant to absorb the flavorful broth while cooking inside the chicken. Along with ginseng and garlic, other ingredients used to flavor the broth include jujubes, chestnuts, and gingko nuts.

Samgyetang is often served with various side dishes such as kimchi, sliced green onions, and salt and pepper for seasoning. The soup is believed to have various health benefits, including boosting the immune system and aiding digestion.
