Bulgogi is probabaly the banchan(side dish) that Korean kids(and I) love the most!

The traditional marinade for bulgogi typically includes soy sauce, sugar, sesame oil, garlic, and scallions, though there are variations depending on the region and personal preferences. Some people add pear, apple, or kiwi to the marinade to tenderize the meat and give it a slightly sweet flavor.

Bulgogi can be served as a main dish with rice and side dishes, or as a filling for lettuce wraps or sandwiches. It is often accompanied by various banchan (side dishes) such as kimchi, pickled vegetables, and steamed or stir-fried vegetables

In addition to beef, bulgogi can also be made with other meats such as pork, chicken, or even tofu for a vegetarian option.
